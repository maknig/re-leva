ReLeva

A coffee machine project

ReLeva is a personal project focused on designing and building a high-performance lever-style espresso machine from the ground up. The goal is to combine the tactile, mechanical purity of classic lever machines with modern control, measurement, and repeatability.

The machine is constructed primarily from stainless steel, with a glass water reservoir, and uses a custom-designed electronics and control system.

Design goals

  • Preserve the mechanical feel and feedback of a traditional lever machine
  • Stable and repeatable brew temperatures
  • Robust and serviceable construction
  • High-end, food-safe materials

Design choices

ReLeva uses 304/316 stainless steel for its boilers and primary thermal components. This material was selected for its food safety, corrosion resistance, and long-term durability. Compared to copper or aluminum, stainless steel provides higher thermal mass, resulting in a slower heat-up phase but improved temperature stability once operating conditions are reached. In practice, warm-up time remains competitive with — and often faster than — many traditional lever machines.

Traditional lever espresso machines typically operate without a pump, favoring mechanical simplicity and purity. ReLeva intentionally diverges from this approach by incorporating a pump to manage the two boilers independently. This enables automatic boiler filling and water-level control, improving usability and safety.

In pump-less systems, boiler refilling often relies on fixed water plugs, requiring the boiler to cool down before opening — an operational compromise that interrupts workflow. By integrating a pump, ReLeva preserves the manual lever extraction experience while removing these practical limitations.

Features

  • Dual boiler architecture: Separate boilers for brew water and steam, each temperature-controlled independently.
  • Constant-temperature brewhead: The brewhead is kept at a stable temperature by circulating water from the boiler via convection, ensuring consistent extraction.
  • Custom controller: Precise temperature measurement and control, plus water-level management.
  • Bluetooth connectivity: Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) for real-time monitoring and configuration.

Controller

ReLeva uses a custom-designed controller board responsible for PID temperature control, boiler water-level regulation, and power management. The design prioritizes robustness and measurement accuracy.

  • Microcontroller
    STM32L431 (ARM Cortex-M4), selected for low power consumption and high-quality analog peripherals.
  • Heater control
    AC heater control with dedicated zero-cross detection.
  • Analog front-end
    Sensor linearization and signal conditioning implemented using op-amp circuit.
  • Connectivity
    Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) for real-time monitoring and configuration.

Known limitations

  • Warm-up time: Due to the high thermal mass of the stainless steel boilers and group, initial warm-up is slower compared to low-mass copper or aluminum systems. The design prioritizes stability over minimum warm-up time.
  • Manual profiling: Pressure profiling is currently purely mechanical via the lever. While this preserves the traditional lever experience, it limits repeatability compared to actively controlled pressure systems.
  • Early-stage firmware: The control firmware is functional but still evolving. Features such as advanced logging, fault diagnostics, and calibration tooling are under active development.
  • Single-sensor brew temperature: Brew temperature control is currently based on a limited number of sensors. A dedicated brew pressure sensor is in development to improve extraction profiling and data correlation.
  • Not production-hardened: This machine is a one-off prototype. Mechanical tolerances, wiring, and safety systems are designed for experimentation and learning rather than certification or mass production.

Project status

ReLeva is an ongoing project. Hardware revisions, firmware improvements, and documentation updates are actively in progress. A pressure sensor is currently in development to profile pressure and temperature during coffee extraction.
